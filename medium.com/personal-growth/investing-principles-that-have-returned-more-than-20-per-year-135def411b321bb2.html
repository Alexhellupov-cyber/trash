Investing Principles That Have Returned More Than 20% Per Year

Dan Pedersen
Nov 25, 2020 · 3 min read

I got interested in the stock market around ten-years ago. And during that time I’ve been fortunate to learn a lot about investing, and managed to have some success with it.

For the first five-years I didn’t buy anything. I just watched and learned, casually. Then I played around with different investment strategies for a couple of years (and lost some money doing it) until I eventually settled on an investment philosophy that lead to an average return of more than 20% per year.

Below is a screenshot from my brokerage account (dated October 31, 2020). The 5-year number includes one of the bad years I mentioned above, before I changed my strategy:

To put the above numbers into perspective:

  • The average investor in stocks averages about a 4% return per year.
  • The S&P 500, which is the bench-mark index by which investors measure their performance, has averaged a return of approximately 10% per year.
  • The best investors in the world typically average 20% to 30% per year over a period of decades.

I’ve adopted my investment philosophy primarily from Warren Buffett, who is widely recognized as the greatest stock market investor of all time. His business partner Charlie Munger and mutual fund legend Peter Lynch have also left big impressions on me. John Templeton also deserves notable mention. I’ve studied them in earnest over the past several years and have taken their wisdom to heart.

The idea is to identify companies that will become more valuable over time. To borrow a term from Buffett, it’s about identifying companies that have a “durable competitive advantage”. In other words, it’s about investing in companies that have an advantage over their competitors and that are able to sustain that advantage over a long period of time.

The idea is to buy such companies at a fair price (or at a bargain, if you can), and hang on to them for a long time. Ideally, until they lose their competitive advantage or until you find something better.

If you’d like to learn more about what I’m doing, you can. I’ve put together a document called Principles of Intelligent Stock Market Investing. It details the investment principles I follow. It contains specific details, including what numbers I look for when analyzing a company and some of the intangibles to consider. It also lists all the companies I invested in over approximately a 4-year period and why I thought they had a durable competitive advantage. Current holdings aren’t mentioned.

Also, I did some research earlier this year and realized that I could have averaged 40% per year if I had merely tweaked a couple of the principles I had been following. Those are also mentioned in the document. I’m currently experimenting with that new approach.
